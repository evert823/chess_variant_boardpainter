# How to Check Your GitHub Copilot Status

This guide explains how to check your GitHub Copilot subscription status.

## Checking Your Copilot Status

### Method 1: GitHub Settings (Web)

1. Go to [GitHub](https://github.com)
2. Click on your profile picture in the top-right corner
3. Select **Settings**
4. In the left sidebar, click on **Copilot**
5. You'll see your Copilot subscription status, including:
   - Whether Copilot is active or inactive
   - Your subscription plan (Individual, Business, or Enterprise)
   - Billing information (if applicable)

### Method 2: Direct Link

Visit: [https://github.com/settings/copilot](https://github.com/settings/copilot)

### Method 3: VS Code/IDE

If you're using GitHub Copilot in your IDE:

1. **VS Code:**
   - Click on the Copilot icon in the status bar (bottom-right)
   - Click "Check Status" or "Copilot Settings"
   - This will show if Copilot is active and working

2. **Visual Studio:**
   - Go to **Tools** → **Options** → **GitHub Copilot**
   - Your status will be shown here

### Method 4: Command Line (GitHub CLI)

If you have GitHub CLI installed:

```bash
gh auth status
```

This will show your authentication status and whether Copilot features are available.

## What You Can See in Copilot Settings

- **Subscription Status**: Active or Inactive
- **Plan Type**: Individual ($10/month or $100/year) or Business/Enterprise
- **Usage Statistics**: Some plans show usage information
- **Allowed/Blocked Suggestions**: Configure which suggestions Copilot should make
- **Content Exclusions**: Files or repositories where Copilot should be disabled

## Troubleshooting

If you don't see a Copilot section in your settings:
- You may not have an active Copilot subscription
- You may need to join a waitlist (if applicable)
- Your organization may need to enable Copilot for you (for Business/Enterprise)

## Getting Started with Copilot

If you don't have Copilot yet:
1. Visit [https://github.com/features/copilot](https://github.com/features/copilot)
2. Click "Start free trial" or "Subscribe"
3. Follow the setup instructions for your IDE
